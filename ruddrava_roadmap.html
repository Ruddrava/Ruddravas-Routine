Your gateway to subsidised, high-quality UPSC coaching in Kolkata — without going to Delhi.

What is SNTCSSC? A state government initiative under the NSATI campus, Salt Lake, designed to train West Bengal students for IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS and allied services. Coaching is heavily subsidised and conducted in collaboration with top institutes like Shubhra Ranjan IAS and Dhyeya IAS.

Eligibility & Requirements
Academic
Who Can Apply
Graduate (or appearing for final year by 1st August of the admission year). Any stream. No minimum percentage specified for the central SNTCSSC. District centres may vary.
Age Limit
UPSC CSE Age Norms
General: 21–32 years (6 attempts). OBC: up to 35 (9 attempts). SC/ST: up to 37 (unlimited). Since you're 23, you have time — but start early.
Domicile
West Bengal Residents
Priority given to West Bengal domicile candidates. Hostel facility available for outstation students at the Salt Lake campus.
Key Constraint
Limited Seats
Admission is first-come-first-served after clearing the screening/admission test. Do not wait — apply as soon as notification drops.
How to Get In — Step by Step
1
Watch for Notification
Check csscwb.in and major Bengali newspapers (Anandabazar, Telegraph) in Oct–Nov for the annual admission cycle. District centre notifications come separately via district websites.
2
Pay Admission Test Fee Online
₹100 (General) / ₹50 (SC/ST/OBC). Bank: Indian Bank, Salt Lake GD Market Branch. Account: Centre for Excellence in Public Management. Pay via NEFT/UPI before filling the form.
3
Fill Online Application Form
One form per email ID. Cannot be revised after submission. Have scanned photo, signature, and all documents ready. Submit on the official portal link given in the notification.
4
Appear for Screening/Admission Test
Written test (100 marks) + possible interview (50 marks) for district centres. Central SNTCSSC conducts its own admission test. Syllabus is general aptitude and current affairs.
5
Join the Composite Course
10-month offline programme covering Prelims + Mains + Mock Tests. Optional subjects also offered. Conducted at NSATI Campus, FC Block, Sector III, Salt Lake, Kolkata 700106.

Indian national. Bachelor's degree (or appearing in final year) in any stream with Mathematics as a compulsory subject in at least one year/semester. BCA holders — check: does your IGNOU BCA include Maths? Most do via BCS-012 or equivalent. General: 50% aggregate. Final year students eligible.

Preparation Roadmap — 12 Month Plan
Months 1–2 — Foundation
Maths & CS Fundamentals
Revise: Sets, Relations, Functions, Algebra, Matrices, Coordinate Geometry, Permutations & Combinations, Probability, Statistics. For CS: Data Structures, Algorithms basics, DBMS, OS concepts. Sources: R.S. Aggarwal MCA Guide, your BCA textbooks.
Months 3–5 — Core Build
Deep Dive into Weak Areas
Identify weak sections through mock tests. NIMCET: Maths carries ~40% — cannot afford to ignore it. Discrete Maths (NIMCET) is important. English: practice reading comprehension and grammar. Logical Reasoning: 30 mins/day consistently beats cramming.
Months 6–9 — Practice Phase
Previous Papers + Mock Tests
Solve last 5–7 years of NIMCET and WBJECA papers under timed conditions. Join free/paid mock series on CareerLauncher or Testbook. Track accuracy per section. Time management at this stage is everything.
Months 10–12 — Final Sprint
Revision + Speed + Accuracy
3 full mocks per week. Formula sheets for Maths. Revise CS fundamentals. Focus on eliminating silly errors. In NIMCET, –1 per wrong answer — do not attempt blind guesses. Aim for 620+ to target top NITs.
Strategy note: Give both exams. WBJECA is easier and keeps options in WB. NIMCET opens NIT Warangal and NIT Trichy — which are significantly more valuable for a Data Science/AI-focused MCA. Aim for both, prioritise NIMCET preparation depth.
