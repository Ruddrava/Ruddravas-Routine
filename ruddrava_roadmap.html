Preparation Roadmap — 12 Month Plan
Months 1–2 — Foundation
Maths & CS Fundamentals
Revise: Sets, Relations, Functions, Algebra, Matrices, Coordinate Geometry, Permutations & Combinations, Probability, Statistics. For CS: Data Structures, Algorithms basics, DBMS, OS concepts. Sources: R.S. Aggarwal MCA Guide, your BCA textbooks.
Months 3–5 — Core Build
Deep Dive into Weak Areas
Identify weak sections through mock tests. NIMCET: Maths carries ~40% — cannot afford to ignore it. Discrete Maths (NIMCET) is important. English: practice reading comprehension and grammar. Logical Reasoning: 30 mins/day consistently beats cramming.
Months 6–9 — Practice Phase
Previous Papers + Mock Tests
Solve last 5–7 years of NIMCET and WBJECA papers under timed conditions. Join free/paid mock series on CareerLauncher or Testbook. Track accuracy per section. Time management at this stage is everything.
Months 10–12 — Final Sprint
Revision + Speed + Accuracy
3 full mocks per week. Formula sheets for Maths. Revise CS fundamentals. Focus on eliminating silly errors. In NIMCET, –1 per wrong answer — do not attempt blind guesses. Aim for 620+ to target top NITs.
Strategy note: Give both exams. WBJECA is easier and keeps options in WB. NIMCET opens NIT Warangal and NIT Trichy — which are significantly more valuable for a Data Science/AI-focused MCA. Aim for both, prioritise NIMCET preparation depth.

IFS — Indian Foreign Service: One of the most prestigious services in India. IFS officers represent India abroad as diplomats, manage bilateral relations, consular work, and international negotiations. It requires clearing UPSC CSE and ranking high enough (top ~35–40 ranks typically) to get IFS allocation. It's an elite within an elite exam.

Stage 1 — SNTCSSC Qualification Exam
The Entry Gate
What the Qualifying Test Covers
The SNTCSSC admission test is essentially a mini-Prelims: General Studies (History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Science, Current Affairs) + Reasoning + English comprehension. 100–150 MCQs, 2 hours. Prepare using: Previous year UPSC Prelims GS Paper 1 MCQs (last 5 years), Lucent's GK, and 3–4 months of The Hindu reading. You don't need coaching to qualify — consistent self-study is enough.

Stage 2 — UPSC Prelims (Qualifying)
Paper 1 — GS
General Studies (200 Marks)
100 MCQs. History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Environment & Ecology, Science & Technology, Current Affairs. Negative marking: –0.66 per wrong answer. Only Paper 1 counts for cutoff. Score typically needs to be 95–110+ to clear in competitive years.
Paper 2 — CSAT
Aptitude (Qualifying Only)
80 MCQs. Comprehension, Logical Reasoning, Basic Maths, Decision Making. Qualifying: 33% = 66 marks minimum. Your BCA background means CSAT is your easiest paper — Maths and Reasoning are strengths. Don't neglect it but don't over-invest either.

History — your Bengali intellectual background gives you natural affinity. Bengal Renaissance, colonial history — you already know this deeply.
Sociology — relatively concise syllabus, scoring, good for Essay paper too.

Recommendation: PSIR — directly feeds into IFS interview and career. Your interest in geopolitics makes this the natural fit.

Stage 4 — Personality Test (Interview)
The Final Filter
275 Marks — Not Just Knowledge
UPSC interview tests personality, not knowledge. The board looks for: clarity of thought, intellectual honesty, balanced views, situational awareness, and communication. Your theatre background is a direct advantage — you're trained in presence, articulation, and thinking on your feet.

For IFS specifically, expect questions on: India's foreign policy, bilateral relations, current geopolitical tensions (India-China, India-Pak, QUAD, BRICS), and your optional subject. Bengali cultural knowledge, Tagore's internationalism, India's diplomatic history — all relevant.
IFS — What Makes It Different
Rank Required
Top ~35–40
IFS allocation goes to the highest-ranked candidates who prefer it. In recent years ranks up to 35–45 have gotten IFS. You need to be in the top 1% of the top 1%. This requires 2–4 years of serious, focused preparation.

The Life
Posted abroad every 3–4 years. Handle visas, bilateral agreements, trade negotiations, diaspora welfare, cultural diplomacy. Languages are prized — German, French, Spanish. Your German learning plan for Germany actually overlaps with IFS career utility.

Bengali intellectual tradition, curiosity about geopolitics and international systems, multilingual interests (French, German you mentioned), theatre and communication skills, strong opinions about India's place in the world. This is the profile of someone who would thrive in diplomacy.
