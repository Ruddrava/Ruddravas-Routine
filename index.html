Dead hang progression — grip & shoulder health
Month 1 — Grip tolerance
Start with 3 sets of 5–10 second hangs. Use an overhand grip, shoulder-width. Let your shoulders shrug up fully — then actively depress them (pack shoulders down). This teaches scapular control.

Rest 60–90 s between sets. Don't force duration. Skin tears are common early — use chalk or gloves.
Month 2 — Build to 20–30 s
Target 3–4 sets of 15–25 second hangs. Add active scapular depressions: hang passively 3 s → retract/depress shoulder blades → hold 3 s → repeat. This is dead hang training AND a lat activation drill.

When 3×20 s feels easy, you're close to a dead-hang pull-up.
Month 3 — 30–45 s hangs
Target 3×30–45 s continuous hangs. Try negative pull-ups if available: jump or step to top position (chin over bar) and lower yourself as slowly as possible — 3–5 seconds down. 3×3 reps. This builds pull-up strength directly.
Why dead hangs matter
Decompresses spine. Builds grip strength. Stretches lats and thoracic region. Corrects rounded posture from prolonged sitting. Conditions tendons for future pulling work (rows, pull-ups, deadlifts). Non-negotiable for desk workers and beginners.
Pushup progression — from zero to full form
Stage Exercise & cues Target
Stage 1 Wall pushups — Stand arm's length from wall. Hands shoulder-width. Lower chest toward wall, push back. Body straight throughout. Perfect starting point — builds movement pattern with zero injury risk. 3×15
Stage 2 Incline pushups — Hands on bench or bar at ~waist height. Same straight body. Move to lower heights progressively. This is the bridge between wall and floor. 3×12–15
Stage 3 Knee pushups — On the floor, knees down. Keep hips aligned — don't let them drop. Chest touches floor each rep. 3–0–1 tempo (3 s down, 0 pause, 1 s up). Build reps before moving to Stage 4. 3×10–15
Stage 4 Negative (eccentric) pushups — Start in full top position (toes, not knees). Lower yourself as slowly as possible (5–8 seconds). Collapse to floor. Reset. Builds full pushup strength rapidly. 3×5–8
Stage 5 Full pushup — Toes, full body plank. Hands slightly wider than shoulders. Lower chest to 1 cm off floor. Push fully up — lock elbows. Never rush this. Quality reps over quantity always. 3×8–10

Rest times between sets
Pushup variations 60–90 seconds. You're a beginner — full rest between sets. Short rest = bad reps = bad habit.
Dead hangs 90 seconds. Grip fatigues fast — give it time. Never do hangs with compromised grip.
Plank & core 45–60 seconds. Core recovers quickly but form matters — don't rush into next set sagging.
Squats, lunges, glute bridges 60–75 seconds. Bodyweight leg work doesn't need long rest, but take it if breathing isn't recovered.
Between exercises 2–3 minutes. Use this time to stretch the previous muscle group, drink water, or do mobility work.

Monthly check-ins
Track: weight (morning, fasted), waist circumference, resting heart rate, max pushups, max hang time, treadmill speed at "comfortable" effort. These numbers don't lie. If weight stalls for 3+ weeks — dial in diet, not exercise. You can't out-train a poor diet.
At 93.45 kg, a 500–700 kcal deficit per day will produce ~0.5 kg/week fat loss — the science-recommended ceiling for preserving muscle while losing fat. Never go below 1,800 kcal/day while training. Protein is the single most important macro for body recomposition. Everything else is secondary.
Daily nutrition framework
Protein — non-negotiable
Target: 130–150 g protein/day (~1.5–1.6 g per kg bodyweight)

Sources: eggs, chicken breast, fish (rohu, katla, hilsa), paneer (moderate), Greek yoghurt, dal + rice combo, soya chunks

Spread across 4 meals. Don't try to hit it all in 2 meals — absorption is limited per meal (~40–45 g usable).
Carbs — fuel for training
Don't eliminate carbs. Time them strategically: largest carb meals around workouts (pre + post gym).

Prefer: brown rice, oats, sweet potato, whole wheat roti, banana. Avoid: maida, white bread, instant noodles, biscuits, fried snacks. Carbs after 8 PM minimal — they won't be used.
Sample day — Bengali context
Breakfast 8 AM: 4 eggs (2 whole + 2 white scrambled) + 2 wheat roti + banana
Lunch 1 PM: 150g fish curry/chicken + 1 cup brown rice + sabzi + dal
Pre-gym snack 4 PM: Banana + handful peanuts or Greek yoghurt
Post-gym 7 PM: Protein + light carb (chire/poha or roti + egg)
Dinner 9 PM: Dal + sabzi + 1 roti (no heavy rice at night)
What to cut immediately
✗ Chanachur, chips, namkeen — empty calories
✗ Cold drinks, packaged juices — liquid sugar
✗ Biscuits, parle-g — refined carb bombs
✗ Deep fried street food daily
✗ Excess oil in cooking (2 tsp/meal max)
✗ Late night meals after 10 PM

You don't need to be perfect — you need to be consistent. 80% clean eating beats 100% clean for 1 week then relapse.
Hydration & sleep — the underrated pair
Water: 3–3.5 litres/day. Drink 500 ml before gym, sip during, 300 ml immediately after. Dehydration drops performance by 10–20% and increases injury risk. If urine is dark yellow — drink more.

Sleep: Non-negotiable 7–8 hours. Fat loss happens during sleep — cortisol drops, growth hormone releases, muscles repair. Sleeping 5 hours while training hard defeats 40% of your gym effort. No exceptions.
Beginner mistakes — read once, remember always
❌ Skipping warmup to "save time"
Cold muscles and joints tear. One pulled muscle = 2–6 weeks off the gym. 10 minutes of warmup protects months of progress. The warmup IS part of the session.
❌ Going too heavy too fast
Ego lifting with bad form builds bad movement patterns that cause injuries years later. The goal right now is movement quality, not weight. A 10 kg perfect squat beats a 60 kg ugly squat every time.
❌ Skipping rest days
You get stronger during rest, not during the workout. The workout is the stimulus. Rest + food is where adaptation happens. Training 7 days/week as a beginner = overtraining = regression.
❌ Comparing to others in the gym
The guy benching 100 kg has been training for 4–6 years. You've been at it 2 weeks. Compare yourself to last week's version only. Progress is personal. Jealousy breaks focus and leads to dangerous shortcuts.
❌ Ignoring pain vs soreness
Muscle soreness (DOMS) 24–72 hrs after training = normal. Sharp joint pain, pain during movement, or pain that worsens = stop immediately and consult. Never train through joint pain. Never.
❌ Eating nothing before gym
Training fasted as a beginner tanks performance and increases muscle breakdown. Eat something — even a banana and a handful of peanuts 60–90 min before training. Your gym session will immediately improve.
❌ Inconsistency — "I'll restart Monday"
The biggest gains killer. Missing 3 sessions/week consistently is 40% of the program gone. Results in fitness are 80% consistency, 20% everything else. Show up even when you don't feel like it — especially then.
❌ No cooldown or stretching
Flexibility lost during this phase is hard to rebuild. Tight hip flexors, tight pecs, tight hamstrings — these cause chronic postural issues and pain. 8 minutes of stretch after training is a tiny investment with massive returns over 6 months.
