Study phases
Phase 1 — Foundation · Jan 1 – Feb 15, 2027
Post-December exams, take a proper week off first — you'll have just finished a heavy 8-subject cycle. Then start light SLM reading across all 4 subjects.

MCS-207 (DBMS): ER diagrams, relational model, normalization, SQL basics. This is the conceptual phase — understand the "why" before you memorise anything.

MCS-208 (Data Structures): Arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, trees. You cleared MCSL-209 (the lab) in the Dec 2026 cycle — connect the theory now to the code you wrote then.
Phase 2 — Main prep · Feb 16 – Apr 30, 2027
Deep intensive study. Run all 4 subjects in weekly rotation — 2 subjects per week in alternating pairs.

Pair A: MCS-207 + BCOS-185 (E-Commerce and Entrepreneurship overlap conceptually — business logic, data management).
Pair B: MCS-208 + MSEI-027 (both involve algorithmic and forensic analysis thinking — different domains, good mental refresh).

SQL practice: Write queries by hand for MCS-207. IGNOU will ask you to write SQL on paper — practice this specifically. Normalization problems are very frequently asked.

DS practice: Draw trees, graphs, and trace sorting algorithms manually. Insertion sort, merge sort, quick sort — know them step by step on paper.
Phase 3 — Past papers + revision · May 1 – Jun 5, 2027
All new material stops. Past papers only — minimum 5 years for each subject.

BCOS-185 and MSEI-027 are conceptual and reading-heavy. Focus on key definitions, case studies, and short answer formats. These are manageable — don't over-invest here at the expense of MCS-207/208.

MCS-207: Normalisation (1NF/2NF/3NF/BCNF), ER to Relational mapping, transaction management, deadlock. These are the repeated exam topics — nail them cold.

MCS-208: Time and space complexity, sorting algorithm traces, tree traversals (inorder/preorder/postorder), graph traversal (BFS/DFS). Again — paper practice, timed.
Phase 4 — Final sprint · Jun 6 – exam start
Revision only. Key formulas, definitions, diagrams. No new papers, no new chapters.
