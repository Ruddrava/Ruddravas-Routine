Study phases
Phase 1 — Foundation · May 1 – May 10
Cover all 5 exam subjects in light rotation. Don't go deep yet — just get familiar with structure and syllabus. Block 1 for MCS-206 and BCS-041 (hardest two). Block 2 for BCOC-131 and BCS-053. Saturday revision for BCS-111 — it's basics, easy to crack later.

Practicals: Get MCSL204, MCSL205, BCSL135, MCSL209 scheduled and done at the centre ASAP. Book appointments this week.
Phase 2 — Intensive prep · May 11 – Jun 5
One subject per week, deep focus.
Week 1: MCS-206 — classes, inheritance, polymorphism, interfaces.
Week 2: BCS-041 — OSI model, TCP/IP, routing protocols, subnetting.
Week 3: BCOC-131 — journal, ledger, trial balance, P&L, balance sheet.
Week 4: All three in parallel, 1.5 hrs each per day. Do past papers every evening.
Phase 3 — Final sprint · Jun 6 – 13
Jun 6–10: Revision only. 1.5 hrs per subject per day. Past papers, key definitions, formulas only.
Jun 11: MCS-206 exam. Evening — skim Networks notes lightly.
Jun 12: BCS-041 exam. Evening — skim Accounts notes.
Jun 13: BCOC-131 exam. Rest completely — you've earned it.
Jun 14 – 17 · BCS-111 sprint (4 days)
Computer basics — your easiest exam. Hardware, software, OS concepts, MS Office, number systems. 4 focused days is more than enough. Past papers + SLM reading. Don't overthink this one.
Jun 19 – 24 · BCS-053 sprint (6 days)
Web Programming — you're already ahead because of your full-stack Udemy course. Focus on theory exam format: HTML tags and structure, CSS properties, JavaScript basics, client-server model, HTTP concepts. Practice writing code on paper. Past papers are essential.
Post-exam · Jul – Aug · Clear remaining backlogs
MCS201 (C & Python), MCS203 (Operating Systems), MCS207 (DBMS), MCS208 (Data Structures), BCS131 (C++) — get these done for the next exam cycle. One subject every 10 days is sustainable. Fitness becomes the primary focus during this phase.
