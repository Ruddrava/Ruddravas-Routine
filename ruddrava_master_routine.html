Study phases
Phase 1 — Foundation · Apr 11 – May 10
Cover all 5 exam subjects in light rotation. Don't go deep yet — just get familiar with the structure and syllabus. Use Block 1 for MCS-206 and BCS-041 (toughest two). Use Block 2 for BCOC-131 and BCS-053. Saturday revision for BCS-111 — it's basics, easy to crack later.

Priority action: Get all practicals (MCSL204, MCSL205, BCSL135, MCSL209) scheduled at the centre and done by end of April. Don't let this drag.
Phase 2 — Intensive prep · May 11 – Jun 5
Now go deep, one subject per week. Week 1: MCS-206 (Java OOP — classes, inheritance, polymorphism). Week 2: BCS-041 (Networks — OSI model, TCP/IP, routing). Week 3: BCOC-131 (Accounting — journal, ledger, trial balance, P&L). Week 4: All three in parallel — 1.5 hrs each per day. Do past papers every evening.
Phase 3 — Final sprint · Jun 6 – 13
Jun 6–10: Revision only. 1.5 hrs per subject per day across all three. Past papers, key formulas, definitions.
Jun 11: MCS-206 exam. Evening — skim Networks notes.
Jun 12: BCS-041 exam. Evening — skim Accounts notes.
Jun 13: BCOC-131 exam. Rest completely.
Jun 14 – 17 · BCS-111 sprint (4 days)
Computer basics — this is your easiest exam. Hardware, software, OS, MS Office concepts. 4 focused days is enough. Past papers + SLM reading. Don't overthink this one.
Jun 19 – 24 · BCS-053 sprint (6 days)
Web Programming — you're already ahead here because of your full-stack Udemy course. Focus on the theory exam format: HTML tags, CSS properties, JavaScript basics, client-server concepts. Practice writing code on paper. Past papers are essential.
Post-exam · Jul – Aug · Clear remaining backlogs
MCS201 (C/Python), MCS203 (OS), MCS207 (DBMS), MCS208 (Data Structures), BCS131 (C++) — get these theory subjects done for the next exam cycle. Fitness becomes primary focus here. One subject every 10 days is sustainable.
At 92.5 kg / 5'9" you're targeting a lean, athletic build — think 72–76 kg by late August. That's ~16–20 kg over 4.5 months (~1 kg/week), very achievable with a consistent deficit + strength training. No gym required.
